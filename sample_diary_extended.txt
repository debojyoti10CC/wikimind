2023-08-12
First week at IIT Bombay hostel. My roommate is Rahul — he's from Jaipur, CS branch like me.
Quiet guy, reads a lot. We stayed up till 3am talking about startups. He has this notebook
where he writes down every business idea he gets. Already filled half of it. I think we're
going to be close friends.

2023-09-03
Met Priya at the entrepreneurship cell orientation. She gave a talk about her gap year —
she spent six months in Bangalore working at a seed-stage fund. Sharp, confident, knows
everyone in the room already. She introduced herself to me after and said she'd heard I was
interested in consumer tech. I don't know how she knew that.

2023-10-15
Arjun transferred into our batch from BITS Pilani. Engineering background, systems thinker.
He and Rahul hit it off immediately — they spent the entire weekend building a small
inventory tool for the college canteen. Just for fun. I watched them work and felt like
I was seeing something real.

2023-11-20
Priya pulled me into a late-night session at the e-cell lab. She's been researching the
quick commerce space — Zepto, Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart. Her thesis is that none of them
have cracked the tier-2 city problem. She thinks there's a company to be built there.
I couldn't sleep after that conversation.

2023-12-05
Rahul showed me his notebook. Page after page of ideas, but one kept coming back:
a B2B inventory management layer for kirana stores that plugs into quick commerce backends.
He called it "the missing middleware." I told him it sounded like Zepto's infrastructure
problem from the inside. He said — exactly.

2024-01-08
New year. Rahul, Arjun, Priya and I had dinner at the dhaba near the gate. We talked for
four hours. By the end of it we had a rough plan: build the B2B layer, call it Zepto
internally for now, raise a small pre-seed from the e-cell fund, and launch in one
tier-2 city as a pilot. Priya said she could get us a meeting with her VC contact in Bangalore.

2024-01-15
Had coffee with Rahul today. We talked about the Zepto pivot — he thinks we should focus on B2B.
I'm not sure. Met at IIT Bombay back in 2019. He's my most trusted person for product decisions.
The conversation went on for three hours at the Blue Tokai in Koramangala. He has this way of
cutting through the noise that I've always admired.

2024-01-22
Working late on Zepto again. Arjun joined the team this week as CTO.
The pressure from the investors is getting intense. Read Atomic Habits again on the commute.
Arjun brings a completely different energy — more systematic, less intuitive than Rahul.
I think that's exactly what we need right now. The engineering culture has been chaotic.

2024-02-01
Goa trip with Rahul and Priya. Needed the break badly.
Priya mentioned she's thinking of joining a VC fund. The conversations at the beach house were
some of the best I've had. We stayed in this little place in Anjuna, right by the water.
Priya has this clarity about the startup world that I find grounding.

2024-02-10
Back from Goa. Arjun had shipped three features while we were gone. He sent a Slack message
at 2am saying "deployed to prod, go check." That's the kind of CTO you want. Rahul called it
"Arjun being Arjun." We're starting to develop our own language as a team.

2024-02-14
Rejected from YC again. Second time. Hard day. Called mom. Arjun said we should pivot to
enterprise — maybe he's right. Started reading Zero to One. Can't sleep.
The rejection letter was kind but firm. Rahul texted immediately — he always knows when
something's wrong.

2024-02-20
Long call with Priya. She's been talking to Sequoia's scout program. She thinks Zepto's
real problem is positioning — we're pitching as infrastructure but we look like a logistics
startup. She suggested we reframe around "merchant intelligence." I wrote that down.

2024-03-05
Long call with Priya about the VC path. She's seriously considering Sequoia's scout program.
I think she'd be incredible at it — she has the pattern recognition and the network.
Talked about IIT Bombay days, how different everything felt then. Simpler problems.

2024-03-10
Arjun built a dashboard overnight that shows real-time inventory velocity for our pilot
merchants in Nagpur. Rahul saw it and went quiet for a minute, then said "this is the product."
I think he's right. The data layer is more interesting than the logistics layer.

2024-03-18
Arjun and I had a real disagreement today about the product roadmap. He wants to cut the
consumer features entirely and go full enterprise. I think we'd be abandoning what makes
Zepto interesting. Rahul sided with Arjun on a call — that stung a little.
But I know they're probably right. Emotions are clouding my judgment.

2024-03-25
Priya introduced us to Vikram — a partner at a small Bangalore fund called Tangent Ventures.
He spent two hours with us, asked hard questions about unit economics. At the end he said
"I want to see your Nagpur numbers in 30 days." That's not a no. Priya was right about him.

2024-04-02
Moved to Bangalore officially. Left Kolkata after six years. Strange feeling.
The apartment in Indiranagar is small but the light is good. Arjun helped me move.
Starting to feel like Bangalore is home now, even if I resist saying it.

2024-04-08
Vikram from Tangent Ventures came back with a term sheet. Small check — 40 lakhs — but
it's real money and a real signal. Rahul cried a little. I've never seen him do that before.
Arjun immediately opened his laptop and started writing the engineering roadmap.
Priya sent a voice note: "I told you so." She was right.

2024-04-15
First team offsite in Coorg. Six people now — me, Rahul, Arjun, and three engineers we
hired from BITS and IIT Delhi. Rahul ran a session on product philosophy. He talked about
the difference between building for metrics and building for people. Everyone was quiet
after. That's the kind of founder he is.

2024-04-20
Zepto hit 10,000 users this week. Small number by any VC metric but it felt real.
Arjun was genuinely excited — first time I've seen him drop the CTO composure.
Rahul sent a voice note at midnight. Priya posted about it on LinkedIn which drove
another 500 signups.

2024-04-28
Vikram made an introduction to Meera — she runs operations at a large FMCG distributor
in Pune. If she pilots Zepto's merchant intelligence layer, it's our first enterprise
contract. Rahul spent a week preparing the deck. I've never seen him work that hard.

2024-05-05
Meera signed. First enterprise contract. 6-month pilot, renewable.
Arjun said "now we have to actually build it." Rahul laughed for the first time in weeks.
I called Priya — she was in a Sequoia meeting but stepped out to take it. That meant
everything.

2024-05-10
Finished Zero to One. Thiel's point about secrets — things you believe that most people
don't — keeps rattling around. What do I believe about Zepto that the market doesn't yet?
I think the secret is that people want tools that feel personal, not enterprise-grade cold.
Started a new note called "The Intimacy Thesis."

2024-05-18
Priya officially joined Sequoia as a scout. She texted: "I'll be watching Zepto from the
other side of the table now." I don't know if that's a threat or a promise. Probably both.
Rahul said it's the best thing that could happen — having a friend inside the room.

2024-05-25
Arjun hired Neha as head of data. She came from Flipkart's analytics team. First day she
rewrote our entire data pipeline in four hours and said "this is cleaner now." Arjun looked
both offended and impressed. I think she's going to change how we think about the product.

2024-06-03
Neha presented her first analysis: our best-performing merchants share one trait — they
check the dashboard before 8am. She called it "the morning ritual hypothesis." Rahul
immediately wanted to build a morning briefing feature. Arjun said we should validate first.
This is the tension that makes us good.

2024-06-10
Read The Hard Thing About Hard Things by Ben Horowitz. The chapter on "the struggle" hit
differently this month. We're not struggling — but I can feel the weight of it building.
Rahul recommended it. He said he reads it every time he feels like quitting.

2024-06-20
Vikram brought in a co-investor — a family office from Mumbai called Meridian Capital.
They want a board seat. Rahul and I spent three days debating whether to give it.
Arjun said "just make sure they add value beyond the check." We gave them the seat.

2024-07-04
Team lunch for Arjun's birthday. He turned 24. He gave a small speech — said Zepto was
the first place he'd worked where he felt like his instincts were trusted. I didn't expect
that. Rahul looked proud. I felt proud too.

2024-07-15
Priya sent a deal memo from Sequoia — not about us, but about a competitor in the same
space. She sent it with one line: "you're 18 months ahead of these guys." I printed it
and put it on the wall.

2024-07-22
Neha's morning briefing feature launched. Day one retention: 67%. Arjun admitted he was
wrong to push back. Rahul said "Neha is the best hire we've made." I agree.
The product is starting to feel alive in a way it didn't before.

2024-08-01
First board meeting with Vikram and Meridian Capital. Prepared for three weeks.
Rahul presented the product roadmap. Arjun presented the engineering plan. I presented
the market thesis. Vikram said "this is the most prepared seed-stage team I've seen."
Meridian's partner said nothing, which I've learned means he's interested.

2024-08-10
Meridian's partner — his name is Suresh — called me directly after the board meeting.
He said he wants to lead the Series A when we're ready. He said "don't rush it."
I told Rahul. He said "now we have to be worth it."

2024-08-20
Priya visited the office for the first time since joining Sequoia. She walked around,
talked to the team, looked at the dashboard. At the end she said "you've built something
real." Coming from her, that's the highest compliment I know.

2024-09-05
Arjun and Neha had a disagreement about the data architecture. It got heated. Rahul
pulled them both aside separately, then brought them back together. By evening they'd
designed something better than either of them had proposed alone. That's what good
leadership looks like.

2024-09-15
Meera renewed the Pune contract and referred us to two other distributors in Maharashtra.
Organic growth. No sales team. Just the product working. Rahul said "this is the only
kind of growth that matters." I'm starting to believe him.

2024-09-28
Flew to Delhi for a conference. Ran into the founder of the competitor Priya had flagged.
He was friendly, asked good questions. I realized we're not afraid of them anymore.
Six months ago I would have been. Something has shifted.

2024-10-10
Rahul told me he's been having doubts. Not about Zepto — about himself. Whether he's
the right person to scale this. I told him the fact that he's asking the question means
he probably is. He didn't look convinced. I'm going to keep an eye on him.

2024-10-20
Arjun shipped the enterprise API. Meera's team integrated it in two days. She called it
"the smoothest integration we've done with any vendor." Arjun forwarded the message to

2024-11-01
Series A prep begins. Suresh from Meridian is leading. Priya is advising from the Sequoia
side — not officially, but she's been on three calls. Rahul is in his element: he loves

2024-11-15
Neha gave notice. She's been offered a VP role at a Series B company. I'm gutted.
Rahul took it hard. Arjun said "we should have given her equity earlier." He's right.

2024-11-28
Neha's last day. She left a document called "What I Know About Your Data" — 40 pages
of institutional knowledge. That's the kind of person she was. Rahul read the whole
thing that night and sent her a long message. She replied: "build something worth
writing about."

2024-12-10
Series A closed. 3.2 crore. Suresh led, Tangent Ventures followed on, and — this is the
part I still can't believe — Priya got Sequoia to put in a small check as a scout deal.
She texted: "now I'm officially on your cap table." Rahul called it "the best day since
the Meera contract." I think it might be the best day, full stop.

2024-12-20
End of year. Sitting in the Indiranagar apartment. Rahul is asleep on the couch.
Arjun is somewhere in Bangalore with his family. Priya is in San Francisco for a Sequoia
offsite. Neha is at her new job. Meera is probably planning Q1 with her team in Pune.
Suresh sent a year-end note: "proud to be on this journey."
I started this year not knowing if Zepto would survive. I'm ending it not knowing how
